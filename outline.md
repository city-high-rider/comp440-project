COMP440 Direct Individual Study
Theorem proving in Idris 2
T1 2026

COMP440 is a direct individual study course, and in this case is on a particular topic of Theorem proving in Idris 2. Idris 2 is a programming language bridging between Haskell and Coq/Agda, thus allowing to make formal proofs like in Coq but with a more programmer targeted approach.

Assessments
The course does not have a final exam, and will be assessed via the following items:

· Assignment 1:
  - Perform a proof about non trivial recursively defined data structures in Idris 2
  - Perform the same conceptual proof in either Coq or Agda
  - Produce a report explaining the features used and comparing the two experiences and artefacts
  [35% of the final grade, submission by start of week 7]
 
· Assignment 2: 
  - Use Idris 2 as a conventional programming language to implement a demo program and verify properies of this program using the proof capabilities of Idris 2.
  - Use Haskell to implement the same conceptual program and to verify as much as possible of similar properties.
  - Produce a report explaining the features used and comparing the two experiences and artefacts
  [35% of the final grade, submission by start of week 10]

· Final presentation: 
  - The students will present their work in individual 1 hour presentations to both the supervisor and another Lecturer.
  - The students will make the presentation into an actual lecture intended to teach those approaches from scratch.
  [30% of the final grade, presentation in the assessment period]

Any changes of the assessments including submission deadlines will need to be approved by the supervisor.
